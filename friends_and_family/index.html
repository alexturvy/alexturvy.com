Why This Version Exists

This is a version of my dissertation written for the people I care about — the ones who've asked me, patiently, what my research is about, who've listened to half-explained tangents about TikTok or Instagram or platform affordances, and who maybe aren't in the habit of reading academic work for fun.

It's not simplified. It's clarified. The big ideas are still here — about creativity, structure, culture, and power — but they're delivered in a way that respects your time and invites you in.

This project uses memes to explore how social media platforms really work — not just what features they offer, but how they shape what people make, how they're seen, and who gets to be heard. It's about TikTok and Instagram, yes — but also about the systems quietly shaping online culture.

The cartoon on this page captures something familiar: even when you're trying to be clear, it's easy to forget what other people don't already know. This document is my attempt to remember that — and to offer an explanation that makes sense, even if you've never used the word "meme" in a sentence with "cultural production." This is the version I hope you'll actually enjoy reading.
